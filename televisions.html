Energy-Efficient Televisions

Discover the story behind TV energy consumption in Australia through our comprehensive data analysis.

What type of TV screen technologies are currently available in Australia and which are the most frequent?

LCD (LED) technology dominates with 3,499 units (84%), followed by traditional LED with 353 units (9%) and premium OLED with 283 units (7%).

What screen sizes are currently available, and which are the most frequent?

The most popular screen sizes are in the mid-range categories, with 23-27 inch screens showing the highest frequency, catering to diverse Australian household needs.

Which brands have the greatest number of different models?

Samsung leads with over 1,100 different models, followed by Panasonic with approximately 680 models, showing these brands offer the widest variety to Australian consumers.

Which type of screen technology consumes the least amount of power?

LCD technology shows the lowest median power consumption, making it the most energy-efficient choice for Australian households concerned about electricity costs.

What is the relationship between screen size and power use?

There's a clear positive correlation between screen size and power consumption - larger screens consume significantly more energy, with power usage increasing dramatically above 80-inch screens.

What is the relationship between star rating and screen size?

Energy star ratings show some variation across screen sizes, with most TVs achieving 2-4 star ratings regardless of size, indicating consistent energy efficiency standards across the market.
